# Introduction

This handbook defines the responsibilities, expectations, privileges, limitations, recruitment procedures, accountability standards, and operational guidelines for all staff members operating under the authority of the TSB Council.

This document applies to:

* Administrators
* Moderators
* Council Representatives
* Community Managers
* Event Staff
* Future Staff Positions
* Any member granted elevated permissions

All staff members remain subject to the Constitution, Community Rules, Council Directives, and applicable community policies.

---

# Article I — Purpose of Staff

Staff members exist to serve the community.

Staff authority is granted for:

* Community protection
* Community management
* Member assistance
* Conflict resolution
* Rule enforcement
* Event management
* Community growth

Staff positions are not status symbols, rewards, or personal privileges.

Authority exists solely for the benefit of the community.

---

# Article II — Core Expectations

All staff members are expected to:

* Follow community rules.
* Lead by example.
* Remain professional.
* Maintain impartiality.
* Protect members.
* Assist members fairly.
* Respect privacy.
* Follow Council directives.
* Act in the best interests of TSB.

Staff should avoid unnecessary arguments, abuse of authority, favoritism, and conduct that damages community trust.

---

# Article III — Staff Conduct

Staff members are expected to maintain higher standards than regular members.

Staff should:

* Remain calm during disputes.
* Avoid unnecessary public arguments.
* Exercise moderation powers responsibly.
* Treat members fairly.
* Act professionally even during disagreements.

Staff authority should never be used:

* For personal revenge.
* To win arguments.
* To silence criticism unfairly.
* To target members.
* To benefit personal relationships.

---

# Article IV — Staff Accountability

All staff members remain accountable to the TSB Council.

The following may result in review:

* Abuse of authority
* Harassment
* Favoritism
* Corruption
* Misuse of permissions
* Negligence
* Unprofessional conduct
* Actions damaging community trust

The Council may investigate staff conduct whenever concerns arise.

---

# Article V — Recruitment Process

Members interested in becoming staff should contact the Founder or Owner.

Applicants may receive a unique referral or invitation link.

To become eligible for staff consideration, applicants are generally expected to bring at least:

## Minimum Requirement

* 50 active members

through their assigned referral link.

The Council may adjust these requirements when necessary.

Meeting numerical requirements does not guarantee promotion.

Quality is valued more than quantity.

---

# Article VI — Staff Evaluation Criteria

Staff candidates may be evaluated based on:

## Community Contribution

* Helpfulness
* Activity
* Reliability
* Positive behavior

## Recruitment Performance

* Number of active members invited
* Retention of invited members
* Community engagement generated

## Trustworthiness

* Rule compliance
* Reputation
* Professional conduct
* Community feedback

## Leadership Potential

* Problem solving
* Conflict management
* Communication skills
* Community involvement

The Council reserves the right to reject any application regardless of referral numbers.

---

# Article VII — Initial Administrator Permissions

New administrators may initially receive limited permissions.

Typical starting permissions may include:

* Delete Messages
* Pin Messages

Additional permissions may be granted later.

Initial permissions should be viewed as probationary authority.

---

# Article VIII — Promotion System

Additional permissions may be granted based upon:

* Performance
* Community contribution
* Activity
* Leadership
* Recruitment performance
* Member retention
* Comparative staff performance

Staff members who consistently contribute to community growth may become eligible for additional authority.

Examples include:

* Managing Voice Chats
* Managing Events
* Managing Community Features
* Expanded Moderation Permissions

Permission expansion remains entirely at the discretion of the Council.

---

# Article IX — Staff Activity Requirements

Staff members are expected to remain reasonably active.

Inactivity may result in:

* Reduced responsibilities
* Restricted permissions
* Temporary suspension
* Removal from staff positions

The Council may determine acceptable activity standards according to community needs.

---

# Article X — Demotions & Staff Removal

Administrative positions are privileges and not permanent entitlements.

The Council may:

* Restrict permissions
* Suspend permissions
* Remove permissions
* Demote staff members
* Remove staff members

when necessary.

Possible reasons include:

* Inactivity
* Misconduct
* Abuse of authority
* Poor performance
* Community disruption
* Loss of trust
* Violation of Council directives

---

# Article XI — Conflict of Interest

Staff members should avoid situations where personal interests interfere with their responsibilities.

Examples include:

* Favoring friends during disputes
* Protecting rule-breaking associates
* Using authority for personal gain
* Manipulating moderation decisions

Where appropriate, staff should recuse themselves from matters involving significant personal bias.

---

# Article XII — Staff Reviews

The Council may conduct reviews of staff members when necessary.

Reviews may consider:

* Activity
* Conduct
* Performance
* Community feedback
* Leadership quality
* Compliance with policies

Reviews may result in:

* No action
* Recommendations
* Training
* Permission changes
* Demotion
* Removal

---

# Article XIII — Confidentiality

Staff members may occasionally receive access to information unavailable to regular members.

Such information should not be:

* Leaked
* Shared without authorization
* Used for personal advantage
* Used to target members

Confidential information should remain confidential unless authorized otherwise by the Council.

---

# Article XIV — Staff Appeals

Staff members may appeal administrative actions affecting their position.

Appeals should be submitted through official Council channels.

The Council may:

* Review evidence
* Review staff conduct
* Uphold decisions
* Modify decisions
* Reverse decisions

Council decisions remain final unless substantial new evidence becomes available.

---

# Article XV — Final Authority

The TSB Council remains the highest governing authority regarding staff matters.

All staff appointments, promotions, restrictions, suspensions, removals, and reviews ultimately fall under Council authority.

Council decisions regarding staff governance are final.

---

# Final Reminder

Being staff means serving the community.

Good staff members:

* Protect members.
* Build trust.
* Help communities grow.
* Lead by example.
* Put community interests above personal interests.

Authority is earned through contribution and maintained through responsibility.
