# Article I — Preamble

The TSB Council Constitution establishes the fundamental principles, authority, responsibilities, and governance framework of The Sovereign Brotherhood (TSB).

This Constitution serves as the highest governing document within all communities operating under the TSB Council and shall guide the administration, moderation, development, and protection of the TSB ecosystem.

The purpose of this Constitution is to:

* Maintain order and stability.
* Protect community members.
* Ensure fairness and accountability.
* Establish governance procedures.
* Define Council authority.
* Promote long-term community growth.
* Preserve the values and mission of TSB.

---

# Article II — Scope of Authority

This Constitution applies to all communities, groups, supergroups, channels, voice chats, bots, services, projects, events, and platforms operating under the ownership, management, administration, supervision, or authority of the TSB Council.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, this Constitution shall apply to:

* Existing TSB communities.
* Future TSB communities.
* Official TSB projects.
* Official TSB events.
* Official TSB services.
* Official TSB automation systems.
* Official TSB representatives.

Participation in any TSB-operated environment constitutes acceptance of this Constitution and all related policies established under it.

---

# Article III — Core Principles

The TSB Council shall operate according to the following principles:

## 1. Brotherhood

TSB exists to create strong communities built upon cooperation, mutual support, and shared growth.

## 2. Respect

Every member shall be treated with basic dignity and respect regardless of personal differences.

## 3. Accountability

All members, staff, administrators, and Council representatives are accountable for their actions.

## 4. Fairness

Decisions should be based on evidence, reason, and community interests rather than personal bias.

## 5. Security

Community safety and protection shall remain a primary priority.

## 6. Growth

TSB shall continuously strive to improve its communities, projects, services, and opportunities.

## 7. Stability

Community decisions should prioritize long-term stability over short-term interests.

---

# Article IV — The TSB Council

The TSB Council serves as the highest governing authority within The Sovereign Brotherhood.

The Council is responsible for:

* Governance
* Oversight
* Strategic planning
* Policy development
* Dispute resolution
* Community protection
* Administrative supervision

The Council acts in the interest of the overall community and its long-term success.

---

# Article V — Powers of the Council

The Council possesses the authority to:

## Governance Powers

* Create rules.
* Modify rules.
* Clarify rules.
* Remove outdated rules.
* Create community policies.
* Create administrative policies.
* Establish operational procedures.

## Administrative Powers

* Appoint administrators.
* Remove administrators.
* Restrict permissions.
* Expand permissions.
* Review staff performance.
* Conduct investigations.

## Judicial Powers

* Review appeals.
* Review moderation actions.
* Resolve disputes.
* Interpret policies.
* Issue final decisions.

## Emergency Powers

During emergencies, raids, security incidents, coordinated attacks, platform abuse, or situations threatening community stability, the Council may implement temporary measures necessary to protect the community.

Such measures may include:

* Temporary restrictions.
* Emergency moderation actions.
* Temporary policy changes.
* Security procedures.

Emergency measures should remain proportionate to the threat presented.

---

# Article VI — Community Governance

All communities operating under the TSB Council shall follow:

1. The Constitution.
2. Community Rules.
3. Community Policies.
4. Staff Policies.
5. Official Council Directives.

In the event of a conflict between documents, the order of authority shall be:

1. Constitution
2. Official Council Decisions
3. Community Rules
4. Community Policies
5. Administrative Procedures

---

# Article VII — Staff Authority

The Council may appoint staff members to assist in the operation of communities.

Staff positions exist to serve the community and are not personal privileges.

Staff members are expected to:

* Act professionally.
* Follow community rules.
* Maintain community safety.
* Assist members fairly.
* Respect Council authority.

The Council may review, restrict, suspend, demote, or remove staff members when necessary.

---

# Article VIII — Member Rights

Members retain the right to:

* Participate in community discussions.
* Appeal moderation actions.
* Report misconduct.
* Request clarification of rules.
* Be treated fairly and respectfully.
* Receive reasonable consideration during disputes.

Membership within TSB communities does not guarantee unrestricted access, immunity from moderation, or immunity from Council decisions.

---

# Article IX — Appeals & Due Process

Members who believe an action was unfair may submit an appeal through official channels designated by the Council.

The Council may:

* Review evidence.
* Request additional information.
* Consult involved parties.
* Uphold decisions.
* Modify decisions.
* Reverse decisions.

Council decisions are considered final unless substantial new evidence becomes available.

---

# Article X — Staff Accountability

All staff members remain subject to the same rules applicable to community members.

The following may result in staff review:

* Abuse of authority.
* Favoritism.
* Harassment.
* Misuse of permissions.
* Conduct damaging community trust.
* Actions contrary to TSB interests.

The Council may investigate staff conduct and take corrective action where necessary.

---

# Article XI — Community Protection Clause

Not every possible situation can be anticipated within a written document.

The Council reserves the right to take reasonable and proportionate action against conduct that harms:

* Community members.
* Community operations.
* Community safety.
* Community reputation.
* Community stability.
* TSB projects and services.

This authority may be exercised even when specific behavior is not explicitly listed within written rules or policies.

---

# Article XII — Official Communications

Only communications issued through:

* Official Council channels.
* Authorized representatives.
* Official TSB services.
* Official TSB bots.
* Official TSB announcements.

shall be considered official positions, directives, policies, or announcements of the TSB Council.

Members should verify information before acting upon unofficial claims.

---

# Article XIII — Financial Independence & Liability

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, TSB communities do not guarantee, endorse, insure, or assume responsibility for transactions conducted between members.

Any financial transaction, investment, trade, purchase, donation, service agreement, loan, or business arrangement involving parties not officially owned or operated by the TSB Council is undertaken entirely at the participant's own risk.

The Council shall not be held liable for losses, disputes, scams, fraud, service failures, or related consequences arising from such activities.

---

# Article XIV — Constitutional Amendments

The TSB Council reserves the right to amend, modify, expand, reorganize, clarify, or replace portions of this Constitution when necessary.

Amendments may be introduced for:

* Community growth.
* Security improvements.
* Operational requirements.
* Legal considerations.
* Administrative improvements.
* Governance modernization.

Where practical, significant amendments may be communicated to the community through official channels.

Continued participation within TSB communities constitutes acceptance of the most current version of this Constitution.

---

# Article XV — Ratification

This Constitution is hereby adopted as the foundational governing document of The Sovereign Brotherhood and all communities operating under the authority of the TSB Council.

All subordinate policies, rules, procedures, and governance frameworks derive their authority from this Constitution.
