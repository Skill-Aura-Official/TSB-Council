# Introduction

The purpose of this document is to establish a fair, transparent, and consistent process for reviewing disputes, moderation actions, staff decisions, and community conflicts within TSB communities.

The TSB Council recognizes that mistakes can occur and that members deserve an opportunity to present their perspective when they believe an action was unfair.

This policy applies to:

* Warnings
* Mutes
* Restrictions
* Kicks
* Bans
* Staff Actions
* Community Disputes
* Administrative Decisions
* Council Matters (where applicable)

---

# Article I — Right to Appeal

Members may appeal moderation actions when they genuinely believe:

* A mistake was made.
* Evidence was inaccurate.
* Rules were misapplied.
* Circumstances were misunderstood.
* Punishments were excessive.

An appeal is a request for review, not a guarantee of reversal.

---

# Article II — Official Appeal Channel

All appeals should be submitted through:

## Council Support

**@TSB_Council_Support**

Members should avoid:

* Public arguments
* Appeal spam
* Harassing staff members
* Creating community drama regarding appeals

Appeals should remain professional and respectful.

---

# Article III — Information Required

When submitting an appeal, members should provide:

## Basic Information

* Username
* User ID (if available)
* Community involved
* Date of incident

## Action Being Appealed

Examples:

* Warning
* Mute
* Restriction
* Kick
* Ban
* Staff Action

## Explanation

A clear explanation of:

* What happened
* Why the member believes the action was unfair
* Any relevant context

Providing complete information helps speed up the review process.

---

# Article IV — Evidence

Members may submit evidence supporting their appeal.

Examples include:

* Screenshots
* Chat logs
* Voice Chat recordings (when authorized)
* Messages
* Other relevant documentation

Evidence should be genuine and unaltered.

Submission of fabricated evidence may result in additional disciplinary action.

---

# Article V — Council Review Process

Upon receiving an appeal, the Council may:

* Review evidence
* Review moderation logs
* Review staff actions
* Review community records
* Request additional information
* Speak with involved parties
* Consult staff members

The Council is not obligated to disclose internal review procedures.

---

# Article VI — Possible Outcomes

After review, the Council may issue one of the following outcomes:

## Appeal Denied

The original action remains unchanged.

## Appeal Partially Accepted

Certain aspects of the action may be modified.

## Appeal Accepted

The original action may be removed or reversed.

## Punishment Reduced

The punishment may be shortened or lessened.

## Punishment Removed

The punishment may be fully removed.

## Administrative Clarification

The Council may provide clarification without modifying the action.

## Escalated Action

In rare situations where serious new information is discovered, the Council may increase disciplinary action.

---

# Article VII — False Reports & False Accusations

Members are encouraged to report genuine concerns.

However, the following are prohibited:

* Knowingly false reports
* Fabricated evidence
* Manipulated screenshots
* Forged conversations
* Malicious accusations
* Misuse of reporting systems

Attempting to weaponize the appeals process against other members or staff may result in disciplinary action.

---

# Article VIII — Staff Appeals

Staff members may appeal:

* Demotions
* Suspensions
* Permission restrictions
* Staff removals
* Administrative actions affecting their position

Staff appeals follow the same review process outlined in this document.

The Council may request additional information when reviewing staff-related matters.

---

# Article IX — Community Disputes

Not all disputes involve moderation actions.

Members may request Council assistance regarding:

* Member conflicts
* Serious disagreements
* Community-related disputes
* Harassment concerns
* Ongoing issues affecting community stability

The Council may act as a neutral reviewer when appropriate.

---

# Article X — Confidentiality

Appeals often involve sensitive information.

Members are expected to respect confidentiality.

The Council may withhold:

* Internal discussions
* Staff deliberations
* Sensitive evidence
* Private information

when necessary to protect individuals, investigations, or community interests.

---

# Article XI — Abuse of the Appeals System

The appeals process exists to ensure fairness.

It should not be used to:

* Harass staff
* Delay punishments
* Create drama
* Target members
* Spam Council representatives

Repeated abuse of the appeals process may result in restrictions.

---

# Article XII — Final Authority

The TSB Council serves as the highest appellate authority within TSB communities.

After review, the Council may:

* Uphold decisions
* Modify decisions
* Reverse decisions
* Clarify decisions

Council decisions are considered final unless substantial new evidence becomes available.

---

# Article XIII — Good Faith Principle

The Council encourages all members to approach appeals honestly and respectfully.

The goal of the appeals process is not to "win" arguments.

The goal is to ensure:

* Fairness
* Accuracy
* Accountability
* Community stability

Members who engage with the process in good faith will receive fair consideration.

---

# Final Statement

A healthy community requires accountability for both members and staff.

The appeals process exists to ensure that moderation actions remain fair, reasonable, and consistent with TSB values.

Every appeal will be reviewed according to available evidence, community interests, and the principles established by the TSB Council.
